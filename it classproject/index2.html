Milan Parajuli

Social Worker

A Passion for Service

Milan Parajuli is a social worker from Nepal with years of experience in the field. He is dedicated to serving his community and making a positive impact in the lives of those around him.

Parajuli began his journey in social work by taking NCC training in 2072. This training gave him the foundation he needed to begin his career in community service. He then went on to work for the NCCAA Palpa District Committee for three years, gaining valuable experience working with different security forces in the COVID period and educating the public about the importance of safety during the pandemic.

Parajuli continued to take different trainings to enhance his skills, including disaster management, fire safety, earthquake rescue, and first aid. These trainings gave him the knowledge and expertise he needed to work in the front line, helping to coordinate relief efforts during natural disasters and other emergencies.

In recognition of his dedication and hard work, Parajuli was recently promoted to the chief of the NCCAA Lumbini Province. In this role, he is responsible for overseeing the organization's operations in the region, as well as implementing new programs and initiatives to help improve the lives of the people he serves.

Parajuli's work has not gone unnoticed, and he has received numerous awards and accolades for his contributions to the community. But for Parajuli, the real reward comes from seeing the positive impact he has made in the lives of those around him. Whether he is working to promote COVID safety or coordinating relief efforts during a natural disaster, Parajuli is driven by a deep passion for service and a desire to help others.
